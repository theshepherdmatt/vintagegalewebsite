The Restoration Process

Restoring a Gale GS401 is not a casual undertaking. Each speaker receives meticulous attention, with every component inspected, tested, and either preserved or replaced with period-correct parts. This is restoration, not reimagination.

1

Initial Assessment

Every restoration begins with a comprehensive inspection. We photograph and document the condition of the cabinets, drivers, crossovers, and hardware. This assessment identifies all issues and establishes a restoration plan.

We test the drivers individually, measure crossover component values, and inspect the cabinet for structural integrity. This stage determines the scope of work required to return the speaker to original specification.

2

Cabinet Restoration

Cabinet work is critical to both appearance and acoustic performance. We carefully strip and refinish walnut veneers, or polish and restore chrome caps to their original mirror finish.

Any damage to the sealed enclosure is addressed with precision joinery. The goal is always to preserve original wood and hardware where possible, intervening only where necessary to ensure structural integrity.

3

Driver Refurbishment

The dual 8-inch woofers and soft-dome tweeter are the heart of the GS401. We inspect surrounds, voice coils, and spiders for deterioration. Where necessary, drivers are re-coned or rebuilt to original specifications.

Each driver is tested on the bench to ensure proper impedance, sensitivity, and frequency response. Only when they meet original factory specifications do they return to the cabinet.

4

Crossover Rebuild

Capacitors drift, resistors corrode, and inductors can fail. We rebuild the crossover network using high-quality components that match the original design values. Every solder joint is inspected and redone if necessary.

The crossover is the interface between amplifier and drivers. Getting it right is non-negotiable. We use period-appropriate component types to preserve the original sonic character.

5

Final Testing & Measurement

Before a restored pair leaves the workshop, we conduct extensive listening tests and acoustic measurements. Frequency response, impedance curves, and sensitivity are verified against original factory specifications.

We run each speaker for several hours to ensure reliability. Only when they meet our standards—and the standards set by Gale in the 1970s—are they ready for delivery.

6

Packaging & Handover

Restored speakers are carefully packaged for safe transport. We provide detailed documentation of all work performed, including photographs and test measurements.

Each restoration includes a written report detailing replaced components, refinishing work, and performance verification. Your GS401s return not just restored, but documented for future owners.
