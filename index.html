The Gale GS401

A British loudspeaker icon from the early 1970s, restored and preserved with absolute respect for its original design, engineering, and sound.

The GS401

Designed in 1973, the Gale GS401 is recognised for its unconventional form, wide bandwidth, and natural tonal balance. Its wrapped cabinet, chrome end caps, and dome midrange made it instantly distinctive — visually and sonically.

Restoration Philosophy

Vintage Gale restorations prioritise originality, accuracy, and longevity. Where original components can be retained, they are. Where replacement is unavoidable, parts are chosen to match original specifications as closely as possible.

No design changes. No modern voicing. No cosmetic shortcuts.

Design Heritage

The GS401 emerged from a period when loudspeaker design prioritised musical realism over specification-driven marketing. Its proportions, materials, and engineering reflect a time when industrial design and acoustic design were inseparable.
