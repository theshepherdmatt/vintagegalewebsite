The GS401 Models

The Gale GS401 was produced in three distinct variations, each with its own character and following. From the iconic chrome caps to the rare walnut finish, these speakers represent the pinnacle of 1970s British engineering.

GS401A

The Chrome Icon

The most recognizable of the GS401 family, the GS401A features the signature chrome-plated caps that became synonymous with the Gale brand. Designed by Jon Bannenberg, these sculptural end caps were as much about industrial design as acoustic function.

Key Features:
• Dual 8-inch woofers in sealed cabinet
• 25mm soft-dome tweeter
• Chrome-plated aluminum end caps
• Power handling: 25-100 watts
• Sensitivity: 89dB
• Impedance: 8 ohms

The GS401A was designed to be driven hard by powerful amplifiers, with minimal compression and exceptional transient response. Its sealed-box design provided tight, controlled bass that defied its relatively compact dimensions.

GS401B

The Rare Variant

The GS401B represents a lesser-known chapter in the Gale story. While retaining the fundamental acoustic architecture of the GS401A, this variant featured alternative cosmetic treatments and is rarely encountered in the wild.

Distinguishing Characteristics:
• Modified end cap design
• Alternative grille configuration
• Same dual-woofer sealed cabinet
• Identical driver complement to GS401A
• Extremely limited production run

For collectors and enthusiasts, the GS401B is the holy grail. Its scarcity and unique aesthetic make it one of the most sought-after British loudspeakers from the era.

GS401C

The Walnut Alternative

The GS401C featured a curved grille and refined styling while maintaining the same uncompromising acoustic performance as the other variants. This model represented the final evolution of the GS401 design, incorporating lessons learned from earlier production runs.

Key Features:
• Real walnut veneer cabinet
• Solid walnut end caps
• Identical acoustic design to GS401A
• Same driver configuration
• Preferred by those seeking wood finish

The GS401C proved that the Gale sound was not tied to chrome and modernism. This variant could blend into more traditional listening rooms while still delivering the uncompromising performance Gale was known for.
